It can be a game changing marketing tool having an app for your business or promotion or whatever to drive traffic and revenue.

Mobile Apps, depending on their functionality, can range between R100000 – R1500000 + vat. App’s are usually used for businesses that require clients to access these information and functions on a regular basis – for example: Taxi Services, Courier Services, Food Delivery Services, etc.

Types of Apps that can be developed:

  1. Basic table functionality - This is a good option for businesses who want an app to display basic information. Think about this as a hierarchy: opening screen has big topics and you click one and you now have a set of new lists to click on.

  2. Database driven custom functionality - Development is more complex on this and gets into whether you want the data housed “native” (built into the app) or “dynamic” (built into an online web services). An example would be having hundreds of dog breeds that you want to organize and display differently.

  3. Enhancement or Modification of the device firmware or hardware - This means that you use certain standard functions of the phone, such as the alarm, camera, or flash, and you enhance or modify it through the app.

  4. Fully dynamic apps - These apps are similar to the database driven apps, they rely purely on external information.

  5. Custom utilities - These apps are geared towards allowing the user to input content.

Overview
App Development Process:

  1. The Ideas and Thoughts.

    This is the step after “I want an App”. This where you really get to unpack what you want your app to look like. While basic app development can work for most business, more complex app developments does allow for a higher return on investment.

  2. Functionality Layout.

    Simply elaborating your ideas to the programmer is not enough. You will need to spend time thinking about every detail, including every interface as well as the components within the interface and how these components will interact with one another.

  3. Design.

    Once you have selected your preferred design, only then your app goes into development. This step is important. Remember that slight tweaks and changes are recommended, however, changing an entire concept mid-way through development is not recommended.

  4. Going Live.

    Once the app is built you need to get it onto the app store (iTunes for example), your developer can help with this. You must set up an iTunes Connect account and complete all the information for the app – icons, descriptions, pricing, etc. You will be able to use the account for future apps as the setup is a onetime thing.

    You can track the following with your account: how many downloads, how much income you are making, etc. What’s great is that you are able to generate an income from your app, such as allowing advertising and enabling in-app purchases. Further to this, we offer account manage in terms of checking your PPC or SEO campaigns, that in turn increases traffic and revenue.
